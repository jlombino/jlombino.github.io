For the past 25.7205 years of my life I have always worried more about the things other people find easy and enjoyed the things other people find hard. Data Science?

Chemical engineering involves way less chemistry than you would think. Statistics, linear algebra, and differential equations are truly the name of the game. While I did spend my fair share of time in a laboratory, it doesn't compare to all of the time I spent trying to understand and remember all of the variations of the Navier-Stokes equations. Little did I know how useful some of this would be in preparing me for my future career in data science.

Following school, I had a great opportunity to get hands-on experience with some substantial HVAC systems. Before the pandemic brought the construction industry to a halt, my primary role involved traveling throughout NYC to price and plan-out water treatment services for cooling towers. Following the lead of more experienced technicians, I learned all about how to operate HVAC systems safely and reliably.

The Future?

I am fortunate enough to say that many aspects of the data science curriculum at Flatiron School will not be new to me. A substantial portion of chemical engineering involves modeling empirical data from chemical processes. In addition, I have been using (rudimentary) python to automate my estimating work and analyze my trades for years. While I am beyond excited to explore machine learning and the like, I am equally happy just to be a participant in the Flatiron professional community. I am most interested in working on projects that aim to understand and remedy mental health issues in the future.

If you've made it this far, don't forget to tune in next time where I'll be discussing the reasons every chart should be a pie chart.
